Being lazy and still productive

5 ways to be lazy and still be productive as a student

Achievements are benchmarks for measuring success. Achievements like winning multiple awards describes an individual's ability and the recognition of these achievements leads to various rewards which is why individuals feel pressured to achieve more and be more productive. Lots of students want the prestige of being more productive but do not achieve anything but rather dally in their wishful thinking. This is because most are 'lazy'. To clear things out, I don't mean not doing anything (because that's not going to work, at all), I'm referring to the particular set of individuals who aren't cut out for much work; those who want to spend as little energy as possible these people are often noted as being lazy and apathetic (especially in Nigeria, with our customs, ugh) but all they really want is low stress. I identify with such people and that's why I present to you 5 ways seemingly lazy students can still be productive.

1. First, you have definitely got to have a standard schedule. So, the goal is to achieve more with less stress right. The first step to rolling through is having a standard schedule. This schedule makes it easier to identify tasks that need to be and when they need to be completed. This list of priorities allows us to manage our energy and time and it helps identify free time. Knowing when to work and when to chill makes things less stressful, doesn't it?

2. Next on the list is doing what interests you and I can't emphasize on this enough. That's why it's always important for students to identify their areas of interest when choosing courses of study. Even when going into other activities like extra-curriculars or acquiring new knowledge, we should always do something we vibe well with, this will let you be willing to do it well.

3. My third recommendation will be to have a well-organized environment. By environment, I mean your room, study, work station and even friends. Organizing elements of our daily lives allows us to filter needs and wants, 'usefuls' and 'uselesses', good and bad or top priority and less priority elements. Organizing helps us get what we need with less stress and lets us discard with lower costs.

4. Fourth on the list is watching your health. Good health is usually underrated. Taking good care of yourself with a healthy diet and regular exercises reduces the risks of health issue. Who wants to spend time being sick or getting to appointments anyways?

5. Lastly, self-discipline is key. Keeping your desires for slacking in check is crucial for productivity. We all want an extra hour in bed (some want to wake up whenever) but not doing anything stressful can bring more stress, eventually. I mean chilling too much can do more harm than good as there is a time for everything, naturally. Do you want to work smart and not use energy wastefully while getting results?
