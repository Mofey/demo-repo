SIMPLE TIPS TO IMPROVE YOUR PUBLIC SPEAKING AND BECOME A BETTER PUBLIC SPEAKER

IMPROVE YOUR PUBLIC SPEAKING AND BECOME A BETTER PUBLIC SPEAKER WITH THESE 7 SIMPLE TIPS

Public speaking just as any skill can be improved upon and talking generally just as any tool you can gain mastering of it and use it effectively and efficiently to achieve any goal you desire (either to persuade, defame, negotiate, explain, influence, manipulate or cause confusion).
Public speaking is a skill that needs to be aquired by virtually everyone (employee, employer, manager, student, public figure, pastors, imams, politicians, artists etc.). Most often in life we find ourselves in places, such as; funerals, parties, meetings, events, gatherings, classrooms, workplaces, that require us to speak to group of people, from at least 3 to thousands/millions of people at once or at a different time.

1. Preparation: Nothing can take the place of preparation, so it cannot be over emphasized. Always be prepared. Abraham Lincoln once said “Give me six hours to chop down a tree and I will spend the first four sharpening the axe.” Preparation gives you the room to digest the topic before presenting it.

2. Record and listen to your voice: during the preparatory time, it is always advisable to record your voice using a recorder (if you don’t have one you can make use of your phone). It enables you to listen to yourself and make necessary adjustments if there is need.

3. Get a mirror: prepare in front of a mirror or a group of few people (e.g your family, friends) who can evaluate your performance and give you feedback

4. Speak slowly: during your presentation, enunciate clearly and express yourself confidently, speakly slowly but not too slowly. This gives your voice power and authority and also enables yor listeners to arbsorb and reflect on what you are saying

5. Build vocal power: always exercise and warm up your voice. Memorise a poem you like and recite it with imagination that you are in front of a group of people.

6. Be yourself: you are unique, realize that and develop your personal unique style of presentation.

7. Use practical example: use examples that your audience can easily relate with
